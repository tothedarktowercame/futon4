This is the **first "Peeragogy accelerator" episode** — a new format
where the podcast looks at an existing organisation through peeragogy
lenses rather than discussing peeragogy in the abstract.  ACMI is the
test case, and it's a strong one: the practice has been running ~20
years, the staff don't use the word "peeragogy" in voice (mostly), and
yet the patterns are evident throughout.  Several substantive
findings: subsidised infrastructure as enabling condition (cable
franchise fees), the quality-and-psychological-safety dual-axis
balance, the explicit naming-makes-repeatable rationale for
pattern-language work, and the strongest single corroboration of
**rubric item 2.7 (timeless-practice)** so far in the corpus.

## Section 6 — Operational content

- **6.0a (curating-not-experting) exhibited.**  Katie's whole trajectory is the curating-not-experting stance applied longitudinally — she learned everything from staff, volunteers, and other interns; nobody positioned themselves as the expert.  Now she does the same for incoming volunteers.
- **Newcomer pattern at scale.**  ACMI's news team is a textbook Newcomer-pattern instance: anyone from the community can come in and start contributing.  Graduated entry built into operations.
- **Roles pattern.**  Multiple paid+volunteer roles with deliberate fluidity.  Katie + Jeff describe the role permeability.
- **Wrapper pattern.**  ACMI's three 24/7 channels are wrappers at broadcast scale.
- **Heartbeat pattern.**  Weekly news team rhythm + nightly production schedule.
- **PAR.**  Notably *absent* from this episode — first episode in the run not to invoke or perform a PAR.  Worth tracking: does the accelerator format itself replace the PAR closing ritual, or is it just a one-off omission?

## Section 7 — What would update this prior?

- **Recurrence in 2026 contexts.**  Community media stations across Massachusetts and (less densely) the US.  Concrete civic-scale peer-production-and-peer-learning instances, mostly grown without the peeragogy label.
- **Adjacent vocabularies.**  *Community media*, *civic media*, *PEG access* (public-education-government cable channels), *cable franchise fees*.  Adjacent civic-media ecosystem with its own internal vocabulary.
- **Critical frame.**  James names the political fragility of the funding model: *"the shifting sands of political kind of put it... an administration that's like more pro business, shall we say... they'll push back against expanding that requirement that the cable companies fund a community of [stations]."*  The subsidised-infrastructure model is contingent on civic political will; can be eroded.
- **Form-claims tested.**  ACMI's *workplace-as-haven* register tests rubric item 5.7 (recharge / co-revery).  Different shape from the OGM Quaker-meeting register, but the same family of practice-as-haven.  Worth holding both as facets of the same general claim.
- **Practice-supporting-doctrine analogy.**  ACMI is the *institutionalised-with-funding* case of the analogy — sangha + temple grounds + paid abbot, by analogy.  The practice is supported by infrastructure that has its own funding mechanism, not by volunteer energy alone.  Different from OGM (volunteer-energy + light-touch infrastructure) and from peeragogy proper (volunteer-energy without infrastructure).  Worth holding all three as a spectrum.

## Additions to the prior (ep 8)

- **A1-ep8 (subsidised infrastructure as enabling condition).**  ACMI's existence depends on cable franchise fees siphoned from Comcast / Verizon / RCN by town negotiation.  The Handbook implicitly assumes peer learning sustains itself on volunteer energy; ACMI shows what becomes possible when the practice has a structural funding source.  **Promoted to rubric (see below).**
- **A2-ep8 (culture-fit hiring as practice-preservation discipline).**  Katie names the discipline: hire for culture-fit alongside skills.  The Handbook doesn't discuss organisational mechanisms for preserving practice culture across staff turnover.  Bears on rubric item 3.9 (stewardship-succession) — culture-fit hiring is one mechanism by which ACMI grows its own stewards.
- **A3-ep8 (quality + psychological safety as a both-AND, not either-OR).**  James: *"you have to create an environment in which people feel comfortable making mistakes while at the same time aiming towards high quality programming."*  The Handbook tends to emphasise inclusiveness; ep 8 names that quality-aiming and mistake-comfort have to coexist as a deliberate balance.
- **A4-ep8 (naming a process makes it repeatable — operational rationale for pattern-language).**  Jeff: *"giving a process a name allows us to repeat it often."*  Direct in-voice articulation of why peeragogy's pattern-language work matters operationally.  Bears on rubric claim 2.6 (pattern-language as language not catalogue) — the rationale is repeatability through naming.
- **A5-ep8 (win-win-win as concrete reciprocal-participation pattern).**  Jeff's three-way framing extends rubric item 3.10 (reciprocal participation) into a structural pattern: community partner + ACMI + viewer.  Worth carrying as a candidate refinement to the `peeragogy/reciprocal-participation` flexiarg.
- **A6-ep8 (fifth corroboration of timeless-practice claim).**  Charlotte: *"we didn't invent Pyrogadji, we just kind of dig it out from under rocks in studios."*  Fifth voicing across the corpus (Charlie ep 1, Charlotte ep 4, Joe ep 5, Pete ep 7 via Quaker-meeting analogy, Charlotte here).  Item 2.7 is now load-bearing.
- **A7-ep8 (hive editing as Handbook practice).**  Charlotte: *"we trust each other. So, you know, I'll go in and change something. I don't have to ask anyone... we call it hive editing."*  A specific peeragogy operational practice for collaborative writing — could become a flexiarg in `futon3/library/peeragogy/`.
- **A8-ep8 (the accelerator format itself is a new carrier).**  Direct evidence about how the practice is adapting its carriers — applying peeragogy lenses to existing organisations.  Bears on the carrier-shape decision: this is itself a candidate carrier shape that isn't on the (a)-(e) list yet.

## Promoted to rubric

**A1-ep8 (subsidised infrastructure as enabling condition)** is the highest-leverage single addition; it surfaces an unstated Handbook assumption (volunteer energy is sufficient) with a concrete counterexample.  General-purpose: applies to many practice-supporting infrastructures, not just community media.  Bears on the carrier-shape decision: carrier (e) — practice-supporting infrastructure — becomes more viable when explicit funding mechanisms are part of its design.  Promoted as new rubric item **3.11 The practice's enabling conditions (volunteer energy, subsidised infrastructure, institutional anchoring) should be visible, not assumed.**
