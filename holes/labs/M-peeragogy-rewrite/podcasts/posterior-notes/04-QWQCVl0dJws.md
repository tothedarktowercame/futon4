## Special framing for this episode — the blackbox recorder

Per Joe's framing: **this episode is a 2020 blackbox recording of why
no further Peeragogy Handbook edition shipped.** Joe states directly:
*"We're pretty keen to get a fourth edition of the peeragogy handbook.
I've even drafted a proposal for the fifth edition at one point."*
Howard separately names a regret: *"I've never managed to get the book
in front of students, which I think, given that it's peeragogy and how
much we've talked about students as co-learners, it'd be great someday
to have this peeragogy handbook as a textbook where the students also
get to rewrite it during the course."* Bryan names structural drags:
credentialism, the Tech Lash, scale of online abuse, neoliberal
atomization. Lisa names a cognitive embedding: *"our thinking may be
embedded in a certain way that predisposes us to think of this
hierarchical way."*

Six years on, neither v4 nor v5 has shipped. The conversation —
recorded six years before the rewrite mission started — surfaces the
mechanisms (or rationales) for the stall. The full set of failure
modes named in the episode itself, distilled:

1. **Editing mechanism arrived late and didn't transfer.** Joe: the
   crowdsourced-editing-via-Metacog-reading-group only emerged for v3,
   in 2016. *"We didn't think of that until 2016 to do."* The
   mechanism wasn't formalized as a v4 process; v3 was the
   experiment, and the experiment didn't get repeated.
2. **The book never reached students.** Howard's explicit regret. The
   classroom anchor that would have driven iterative rewriting per
   peeragogy's own logic (students as co-learners who rewrite the book
   during the course) never materialized.
3. **Convener stepped back without successor.** Howard: *"At one
   point I wanted to step away entirely … but eventually I really
   stepped back from the process and the community took over."* The
   community evidently took over enough to continue the practice
   (podcasts, Cool Laboratory, etc.) but not enough to ship the next
   canonical text.
4. **Bryan's structural drags.** Tech Lash, social-media-giant
   anti-sociality, scale of troll armies / online abuse,
   credentialism's "huge power of the expert," neoliberal atomization.
   All of these compress the cultural surface area on which a v4
   would have landed.
5. **Lisa's cognitive-embedding observation.** *"Our thinking may be
   embedded in a certain way that predisposes us to think of this
   hierarchical way."* The collaborative way of being is not the
   default; the default tax compounds.

The episode acknowledges all of these. *None of them are
disqualifying for peeragogy as a practice.* All of them are
disqualifying for peeragogy-as-monolithic-text-production-project.
That distinction matters for the carrier-shape decision: it's
direct 2020-voice evidence that **carrier (a) [chapter-by-chapter
rewrite of the existing Handbook] is the carrier shape that
materially failed for six years.** The other carriers — zines,
hybrid, practice-supporting-infrastructure — are open precisely
because the text-as-canonical-artifact carrier has 6 years of
no-shipping behind it.

## Section 6 — Operational content

- **Roles pattern + scaffolding-and-stepping-back.** Howard articulates the convener role with precision: *"the first duty of a leader is to define reality"* + *"if you set the expectations at the beginning as a leader, and then part two is step back, give them a little room to push back on it to help you understand what they think you're saying and give up some of your authority."* Two-phase: define-reality, then step back. **The episode also exhibits what happens when there's no successor for the stepped-back role — see Additions and the promoted rubric item below.**
- **Wrapper pattern (implicit).** Bryan's monthly FTTE report is a Wrapper at scale: distilling input from many contributors into a maintained overview.
- **Roadmap pattern (implicit).** The repeated reference to "v4" and "v5 proposal" is roadmap-vocabulary applied to the Handbook, even though the roadmap didn't get walked.
- **Heartbeat pattern.** Bryan's Future Trends Forum: weekly video conference for nearly 5 years. Heartbeat at scale.
- **6.0a Curating-not-experting** *(added ep 3)* — partial here. Bryan describes himself as "exploiting friends without paying them," which is curatorial; Howard describes himself as "letting go of my own ideas," which is the curator-stance applied to one's own contributions.
- **NEW pattern candidate: Stewardship-succession.** When a convener steps back, the practice needs a stewardship role to continue producing canonical artifacts. The episode is direct evidence that the absence of this pattern is what stops the next text from shipping. **Promoted to rubric (see below).**

## Section 7 — What would update this prior?

- **Recurrence in 2026 contexts.** Bryan's Future Trends Forum is a striking instance of long-running practice-supporting infrastructure. *Academia Next* (his book) is one carrier of the analysis; the FTTE monthly report is another; the weekly video conference is a third. The same speaker has multiple co-existing carriers, none of which is a single canonical text.
- **Adjacent vocabularies.** *Open Educational Resources*, *open access*, *neoliberalism*, *Tech Lash*, *Web 2.0*, *connectivism* (implicit), *scaffolding* (Howard's pedagogical vocabulary). All adjacent to peeragogy's vocabulary.
- **Critical frame.** Bryan's explicit naming of credentialism, online abuse, neoliberal atomization, and women / minorities receiving disproportionate hostility in online discourse. Lisa's hierarchy-embedding question. The critical frame is in the room with peeragogy, not external to it.
- **Practice-supporting-doctrine analogy.** The episode IS direct evidence for the analogy: the *practice* (collaborative writing, peer convening, FTTE-style ongoing forum) has continued for years; the *canonical text* (Handbook) hasn't shipped a new edition in (now) ten years. Sangha and consciousness-raising both worked the same way: practice outlives any one text.
- **Form-claims tested.** 5.1 (living document) is challenged by 2026 reality. The Handbook isn't living; the practice around it is.

## Additions to the prior (ep 4)

- **A1-ep4 (stewardship-succession is the missing pattern).** Howard names convener-then-step-back; the episode is direct evidence that without an explicit successor for the convener role, the practice continues at reduced scope but doesn't ship its next canonical artifact. The 2016 Handbook's *"polycentric leadership"* claim assumes succession will happen organically; ep 4's six-years-later reality is that it didn't, for the canonical-text production task. **Promoted to rubric (see below).**
- **A2-ep4 (the canonical text is the wrong shape for what the practice actually does).** Multiple speakers across the episode have ongoing carriers that AREN'T books: weekly video conference (Bryan), monthly report (Bryan), FTTE email list, the podcast itself. The book is one carrier among many — and the one that's been sitting still. The carrier-shape decision in DERIVE should weight this.
- **A3-ep4 (Howard's regret = the missing classroom anchor).** Without a classroom adoption, peeragogy's own logic (students as co-learners who rewrite during the course) never closed. The text and its readership were structurally separated.
- **A4-ep4 (define-reality-then-step-back as the convener move).** Howard's two-phase pattern. Distinct from Roles, distinct from Curating-not-experting. The convener's specific move sequence.
- **A5-ep4 (cognitive embedding tax).** Lisa: hierarchical thinking is the default; peeragogy works against the cognitive grain. This compounds across years and accounts for some of the v4-stall.
- **A6-ep4 (recurring "we didn't discover it" claim).** Charlotte echoes Charlie Danoff's ep 1 line. Two speakers in two episodes naming peeragogy as a vocabulary for pre-existing practice. The "timeless-practice" rubric item 2.7 (added from ep 1) is now corroborated by an independent voice.
