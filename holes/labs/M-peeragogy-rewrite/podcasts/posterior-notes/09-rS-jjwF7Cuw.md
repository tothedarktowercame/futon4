## Section 6 — Operational content

- **6.0a (curating-not-experting) exhibited.**  Charlie facilitates lightly between five expert guests; brings each in sequentially; integrates with summaries.  Strong working example.
- **PAR notably absent** — second consecutive episode (after ep 8) without PAR.  Worth tracking: does the accelerator + course format replace the closing PAR ritual, or are these one-off omissions?  Pattern emerging that PAR is bound to the *core-crew-conversation* episodes (eps 1-7) and not to the *outward-facing* episodes (eps 8-9).
- **Newcomer pattern.**  The pilot course IS a Newcomer-pattern instance at scale: graduated entry into peeragogy practice for any participant, with co-learner positioning from day one.
- **Roles pattern.**  Howard names co-learner as a role-shift from teacher/student.  Christy's classroom integration uses role-shifts.
- **Wrapper pattern.**  Sherry's Afro Crowd YouTube tutorials in two languages are wrapper artifacts.
- **NEW pattern candidate: Diversity-as-collective-intelligence-anchor.**  Howard names a research-grounded claim (collective intelligence research): *"it's not the particular expertise of the people in a group that contributes to its collective intelligence. It's really the diversity of the group."*  Direct counter to expert-centric framings; gives the curating-not-experting stance (rubric 6.0a) and the polycentric-leadership claim (3.1) a research mechanism.  **Promoted to rubric (see below).**

## Section 7 — What would update this prior?

- **Recurrence in 2026 contexts.**  Cronkite School journalism program (Christy); Afro Crowd (Sherry); News on Wiki (Pete); the projected pilot course itself.  Multiple 2020s pedagogical experiments adjacent to peeragogy.
- **Adjacent vocabularies.**  *Service learning* (Pete) — formal-education concept where students volunteer with a nonprofit whose mission relates to the course.  *Collective intelligence* (Howard, citing research).  *Civic information ecosystem* (implicit in the News on Wiki framing).  *Public interest as anchor* (Pete).
- **Critical frame.**  Pete on businesses trying to put articles about themselves on Wikipedia: *"unchecked, that's not really a good thing in the Wikipedia ecosystem... but if they're doing that in a context where there are students and professors and experienced Wikipedia editors, a lot of that kind of gets, you know, there's a lot of eyes from different perspectives and really the interest of society in general of having information about its media sources tends to kind of override."*  Critical pattern evaluation in voice.
- **Form-claims tested.**  PAR absent in two consecutive episodes (8 + 9).  Form-change pattern emerging: the accelerator + course episodes don't use PAR.
- **Practice-supporting-doctrine analogy.**  Howard's *"peeragogy is kind of a fancy word. People have done peer learning since forever"* directly supports the analogy: peeragogy-the-vocabulary names something pre-existing and universal, like sangha-the-vocabulary or consciousness-raising-the-vocabulary.
