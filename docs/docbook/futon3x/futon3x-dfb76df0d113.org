* What they are
An AIF head is the sensory-motor interface that turns a peripheral from a tool
(does what the agent says) into an organism (maintains its own viability). Every
peripheral that agents inhabit has an AIF head, implemented as two composing
protocols:

- =AifAdapter= (futon2): =select-pattern= + =update-beliefs= — the core AIF loop
- =AifHead= (futon2): =observe= + =default-mode= + =check-law= — peripheral-grade extensions

Together: 5 methods, three tiers (reflex, default mode, deliberative).

* Why every peripheral needs one
Without an AIF head, a peripheral constrains but doesn't learn. It enforces
phase ordering and required outputs, but it has no generative model, no
prediction error tracking, and no inter-cycle behavior. When a cycle completes,
it stops and waits.

With an AIF head, the peripheral:
- Observes its environment through normalized channels
- Consults structural laws before transitions (refusal surface)
- Tracks prediction divergence between propose and execute phases
- Enters default mode between cycles (PAR, portfolio check, compliance scan)
- Participates in the evidence landscape as both reader and writer

* The three-pillar mapping
The futon stack's three conceptual pillars map directly onto AIF components:

| Pillar | AIF Component | Implementation |
|--------+---------------+----------------|
| The Argument (holistic-argument-sketch) | Generative model | Structural law inventory as queryable pldb |
| The Invariants (structural-crystallization) | Precision (tau) | Operational = high, candidate = low, liquid = negotiable |
| Futonic Missions (futonic-missions.md) | Policy | Action selection to minimize free energy through mission phases |

* Self-enforcing invariant
"Every peripheral has an AIF head" is itself a testable structural law.
=futon3c.aif.invariant= maintains a registry of AIF head bindings and provides
=check-aif-head-coverage= to verify that all registered peripherals have
bindings. See docbook://futon3x AIF Heads / The coverage invariant.
