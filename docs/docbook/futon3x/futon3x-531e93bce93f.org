* Context
futon3 was a monolith doing three jobs at three timescales. It held MUSN
lifecycle management, pattern search and hints, tatami session stores, the HX
hypertext system, real-time transport, agency coordination, and forum threads.
Everything lived in one repo and one process.

The existing futon3 docbook (44 entries) documents that monolith. This chapter
documents what came after: the split into futon3a, futon3b, and futon3c, and the
new capabilities that emerged — portfolio inference, tension discovery, and the
self-representing stack.
